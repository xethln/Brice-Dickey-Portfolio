Case 2: French Promotional Email Analysis

Email authentication, header review, and URL reputation validation.

Email SecuritySPF/DKIM/DMARCVirusTotalFalse Positive Reduction

A promotional email written in French was received in a personal Gmail account. Since unfamiliar language and promotional content can sometimes resemble phishing, I reviewed the original email, authentication results, and VirusTotal reputation.

Final Assessment: Benign / legitimate marketing email.
  • SPF, DKIM, and DMARC all passed.
  • Sender domain aligned with Emma Sleep marketing infrastructure.
  • VirusTotal showed 0 detections for the checked URL.
  • No indicators of malware, phishing, or credential harvesting were found.

This case demonstrates that not every unfamiliar email is malicious. A good SOC analyst avoids assumptions and validates using headers, authentication results, and reputation tools.

Case 3: E-Commerce Domain Legitimacy Review

OSINT investigation of americanmuscle.com after pricing appeared unusually low.

OSINTWHOISDNSMXToolboxVirusTotal

The scenario was based on wanting to purchase automotive parts from americanmuscle.com, but the pricing appeared almost too good. I conducted a passive investigation using WHOIS, DNS/MX records, blacklist checks, SMTP testing, and VirusTotal to determine whether the domain appeared legitimate or suspicious.

Final Assessment: Legitimate website / no malicious indicators identified.
  • WHOIS: Domain was created in 2000, which is a strong trust indicator compared to newly registered scam domains.
  • Registrar/Infrastructure: GoDaddy registrar and Cloudflare infrastructure were observed.
  • Email: MX records pointed to Microsoft/Outlook protection infrastructure.
  • Blacklist: MXToolbox checks showed no blacklist hits.
  • SMTP: Server supported TLS, had valid hostname/reverse DNS, and was not an open relay.
  • VirusTotal: URL scan returned 0 detections.

Low pricing alone is not enough to classify a website as suspicious. Passive OSINT and reputation checks are useful for validating whether a domain has trust signals or malicious indicators before making a decision.
